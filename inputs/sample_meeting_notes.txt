Meeting Notes - Product Discovery Session
==========================================
Date    : 2024-03-15
Project : Internal Knowledge Base Assistant
Present : Product Lead, Engineering Lead, UX Designer, Customer Success Manager

---

Summary of discussion
----------------------
We need an internal tool that lets employees search and interact with company
knowledge spread across Confluence, Google Drive, and past Slack threads.
The current situation is that staff spend 30–45 minutes per day searching for
information they know exists somewhere.

The assistant should feel like talking to a knowledgeable colleague:
you ask a question in natural language, it finds the relevant documents,
and gives you a direct answer with source citations.

Functional requirements agreed in this session
-----------------------------------------------
1. Semantic search across connected knowledge sources
   - Confluence spaces, Google Drive folders, Slack channel archives.
   - Results ranked by relevance, not just keyword match.
   - Support for filtering by source, date range, and author.

2. Conversational Q&A interface
   - Chat-style web UI.
   - Multi-turn conversation: follow-up questions maintain context.
   - Response always includes a "Sources" section listing the documents used.

3. Document summarisation
   - Users can paste a document URL or upload a PDF.
   - The assistant produces a structured summary: key points, action items,
     open questions.

4. Onboarding assistant mode
   - New-hire persona with a curated set of must-read documents.
   - Guided Q&A flow covering company policies, team structure, tools.

5. Admin controls
   - IT admins can grant or revoke access to specific knowledge sources
     per user group.
   - Audit log of all queries (no content stored, just metadata).
   - Ability to mark documents as "restricted" so they are excluded from
     certain user groups.

6. Feedback and quality loop
   - Thumbs up / thumbs down on each answer.
   - Feedback routed to a small content team that reviews and improves
     source documents.

Users identified
----------------
- General employees       : primary users for day-to-day knowledge lookup.
- New hires               : onboarding-focused usage.
- IT administrators       : manage source connections and access controls.
- Content / HR team       : review feedback, maintain knowledge sources.
- Engineering team        : integrate the API in internal tooling.

Open questions (to be resolved before sprint 1)
------------------------------------------------
- Which embedding model to use (OpenAI vs. open-source)?
- Slack archive ingestion: legal has to sign off on retention policies.
- Should answers be streamed or returned all at once?
